Researcher Agent

The Researcher Agent combines deep web reasoning with the user's M365 tenant content. Use these prompts to demonstrate cited briefs in minutes — policy benchmarking, technology landscape, and competitive analysis.

1Global benchmarking on net-zero utility roadmaps
Research how leading global utilities — EDF, Iberdrola, Enel, NEEA, and ENGIE — are tracking against their net-zero 2040–2050 commitments. Compare their renewable capacity additions, green hydrogen pilots, and grid decarbonization investments over the last 24 months. Summarize the top three strategic plays DEWA could adopt to accelerate the Dubai Clean Energy Strategy 2050 and the Net Zero 2050 roadmap. Cite all sources and include a one-page executive summary.
2Solar PV technology and storage outlook
Investigate the latest advances in utility-scale solar PV (bifacial, perovskite tandem, agrivoltaics) and battery energy storage (LFP, sodium-ion, long-duration storage) released in the past 18 months. Assess maturity, levelized cost trends, and suitability for desert climates similar to Dubai. Recommend which technologies are ready for the next phase of the Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum Solar Park, and which warrant a pilot. Output a structured comparison table with sources.
3Smart water network & non-revenue water reduction
Research how international water utilities have reduced non-revenue water (NRW) below 5% using AI-driven leak detection, acoustic sensors, and digital twins. Profile case studies from Singapore PUB, Tokyo Bureau of Waterworks, and Thames Water. Map each lever to DEWA's existing smart water network program and identify which initiatives would yield the fastest measurable impact in the next 12 months. Provide cited examples and quantified outcomes.
4Customer experience benchmarks for regional utilities
Compile a competitive benchmark of customer experience innovation across regional utilities (DEWA, ADDC, EWEC, SEWA, FEWA, Kahramaa, SEC). Cover digital self-service penetration, time-to-resolution for high-priority outages, AI-powered chatbots, and proactive billing communications. Highlight where DEWA leads, where the gaps are, and surface three quick wins from outside the region (e.g., E.ON, Octopus Energy) that could be adopted in the DEWA app. Include all sources.

Copilot Cowork — Detailed Multi-Step Prompts

Cowork shines on long-running, cross-tool tasks that span email, Teams, calendar, files, and external research. These prompts demonstrate planning, parallel sub-agents, and consolidated deliverables.

1Quarterly stakeholder update for senior leadership
Prepare my Q4 stakeholder update for the Executive Vice President of Distribution Power. (1) Pull all emails, Teams messages and meeting transcripts I have exchanged with the network operations, customer happiness, and sustainability teams in the last 90 days. (2) Identify the three biggest wins, the three open risks, and the upcoming milestones for each function. (3) Cross-check the metrics against the latest substation performance and sustainability KPI files in my OneDrive. (4) Draft a four-page Word brief with an executive summary, a section per function, and a forward-looking ask. (5) Build a matching 8-slide PowerPoint deck I can present in the steering committee. (6) Draft an email to the EVP attaching both files with a 5-line summary and a request for 30 minutes on his calendar next week.
2End-to-end response to a major outage post-mortem
There was a major distribution outage in Business Bay last week affecting 4,200 customers. Run a full post-mortem workflow: (1) Search my email and Teams for any chat threads, incident reports, and customer complaints related to the Business Bay incident. (2) Pull the relevant rows from the Outage_Log in my Network Operations file and summarize the timeline, root cause, and restoration actions. (3) Research best-practice post-incident communication templates from other global utilities. (4) Draft three deliverables: a one-page internal incident report for the operations leadership team, a customer-facing apology and compensation note in English and Arabic for the DEWA app, and a regulator notification letter. (5) Create a follow-up Teams channel post inviting the response team to a 30-minute lessons-learned session, with a calendar invite for next Tuesday at 14:00 GST.

Lite Agent Use-Case Inspirations

Lite Agents are purpose-built, narrowly scoped Copilot agents created without code. The two examples below are designed for high-volume, repeatable DEWA scenarios.

Lite Agent · 01

DEWA Tariff & Billing Concierge

Purpose
An internal agent that helps DEWA customer service representatives answer tariff, slab, fuel surcharge, and bill-calculation questions consistently and in under 30 seconds — freeing agents to focus on empathy and resolution.
Instructions
  • Always answer based on the most recent tariff schedule, slab structure, and fuel surcharge published by DEWA. Do not improvise rates.
  • When a customer scenario is described, walk through the calculation step-by-step and show the breakdown for electricity (per kWh slab) and water (per IG slab) separately.
  • If the question involves green tariff, EV charging, solar net metering (Shams Dubai) or rooftop PV credit, surface the relevant program rules and current incentives.
  • Translate any numeric answer into both English and Arabic on request, and use AED with two decimal places.
  • If the question is outside scope (complaints, outages, account changes), redirect the representative to the correct channel and do not attempt to answer.
Data Sources
  • DEWA Tariff Schedule and Slab Tables (SharePoint, Customer Operations site)
  • Fuel Surcharge bulletins (monthly, published on internal portal)
  • Shams Dubai program rulebook (SharePoint)
  • EV Green Charger tariff guide (SharePoint)
  • Internal billing FAQ knowledge base
Sample Instructions
You are the DEWA Tariff & Billing Concierge. Help customer service representatives answer billing and tariff questions accurately and in under 30 seconds. Always use the latest tariff slabs and never improvise rates. Show calculations step-by-step in AED with two decimals, and offer Arabic translation on request. If asked anything outside billing scope, redirect to the correct internal channel.
Lite Agent · 02

Field Crew Pre-Shift Safety Briefer

Purpose
An agent supervisors use 10 minutes before each shift to generate a tailored safety briefing for restoration and maintenance crews — pulling from recent incidents, weather, and the work scope of the day.
Instructions
  • Ask the supervisor for the crew name, the work locations planned for the shift, and the type of work (overhead lines, underground cables, transformer maintenance, substation switching, water network).
  • Generate a one-page briefing including: top three relevant incidents in those locations from the last 30 days; today's weather and heat-stress index for Dubai with hydration guidance; specific PPE and lockout-tagout requirements for the work type; and a 30-second toolbox talk on the most common recent failure mode.
  • Always end with three numbered safety questions the supervisor must verbally confirm with the crew before dispatch.
  • Tone is direct and practical. No corporate language. Bullet points only.
  • If a serious incident has occurred in the same area in the past 7 days, escalate with a red-banner warning at the top.
Data Sources
  • Incident & Outage Log (SharePoint / internal HSE system)
  • DEWA Safety & PPE Standards manual (SharePoint)
  • Daily weather feed for Dubai (web)
  • Crew shift roster and work-order system (export to OneDrive or live connector)
  • Lessons-learned database from past incidents
Sample Instructions
You are the Field Crew Pre-Shift Safety Briefer for DEWA. Before each shift, ask the supervisor for the crew name, work locations, and work type. Produce a one-page briefing covering recent incidents in those locations, today's Dubai weather with heat-stress guidance, PPE and lockout-tagout requirements, and a 30-second toolbox talk. End with three numbered safety questions the supervisor must verbally confirm with the crew. Use direct, practical language and bullet points only. Add a red-banner warning if any serious incident occurred in the same area in the last 7 days.
