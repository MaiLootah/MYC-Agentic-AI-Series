PPrompting Cheat Sheet — read this first

A good prompt has four parts: Role (who Copilot is being), Task (what to do), Context (data, audience, constraints), and Format (length, structure, tone). Get all four in and you'll feel the difference instantly.

TIP 1
Set the role
"Act as my network performance engineer…" or "You are my CHRO speech-writer…" beats a bare question every time.
TIP 2
Be specific about output
Length, audience, tone, format. "1-page memo, formal, for the Executive Committee" tells Copilot exactly what to produce.
TIP 3
Ground it on your data
Reference a SharePoint file, attach a doc, or @mention a Teams channel. Grounded Copilot is 10× more accurate.
TIP 4
Iterate, don't restart
Reply with "make it shorter", "more confident", "في العربية", "add a chart". Copilot keeps context across turns.
TIP 5
Ask it to show its work
Especially with numbers. "Show the formula" / "show the Python" / "cite every source" — audit trail for free.
TIP 6
Give an example of the style
Paste a paragraph in the voice you want. Copilot mirrors style much better than it follows abstract instructions.
TIP 7
Chain, don't cram
For complex work, do one step at a time. "First summarize the report. Now draft an email about it. Now build a deck."
TIP 8
Verify the high-stakes stuff
For numbers, regulatory, or external-facing copy, always read carefully. Copilot is a co-pilot, not an autopilot.

Researcher Agent

Copilot · Deep Research

What it does: Researcher is a Copilot reasoning agent that runs multi-step investigations across the open web and your tenant content (SharePoint, OneDrive, Teams, email). It breaks a big question into sub-questions, searches in parallel, then synthesizes a structured, fully-cited briefing — like a junior analyst that never sleeps.

Best for: competitive intelligence, market sizing, regulatory scans, technology landscapes, partner due-diligence, board-prep briefings.

Prompt — GCC Enterprise Cloud & Managed Services Market Brief 
Act as my market intelligence analyst. Research the GCC enterprise cloud and managed services market through 2030, with a specific focus on:
1. Market size today and projected 2027/2030 spend (in USD billions)
2. The top 5 providers in the UAE and KSA and their announced data-centre capacity
3. Where du Tech (du's enterprise ICT arm) sits today vs e& enterprise, stc business, and Etisalat-by-e&
4. Key demand drivers: government digital transformation, sovereign cloud, AI workloads, 5G edge
5. Risks: hyperscaler direct sales, talent shortages, data-residency regulation, pricing pressure
Return a 2-page briefing with citations and a 1-slide executive summary.
Watch for during the demo:
  • The visible plan: Researcher shows the sub-questions it's decomposing the task into before it answers.
  • Parallel search across the open web + your tenant — citations are clickable and traceable.
  • One-click export to Word or PDF, ready for circulation.
Try these du-relevant variants:
  • "Compare du's stated 5G and AI ambitions with e&, stc, and Ooredoo — coverage, partnerships, monetisation models."
  • "Scan the last 12 months of TDRA and UAE Digital Economy Strategy updates and tell me what changes for du's wholesale and enterprise business."
  • "Map every public partnership du has signed with a hyperscaler or AI firm since 2024, with deal size where disclosed."

Analyst Agent

Copilot · Python Data Analyst

What it does: Analyst is the Python-powered data agent inside Copilot. Upload a CSV, Excel file, or paste a table — Analyst writes, runs, and explains real Python code in front of you. Where Excel shines on neat tabular finance, Analyst shines on messy data: unstructured text, clustering, NLP-on-descriptions, statistical tests, anomaly detection — anything that benefits from real Python libraries.

Best for: network incident analysis, customer-experience analytics, churn modelling, employee survey free-text, customer feedback, performance-data anomaly hunting.

Use case — Q1 2026 Network Incident Deep-Dive 
You are my network operations analyst. Using the attached file Du_Network_Incidents_Q1_2026.csv (95 incidents across seven technology domains), do the following:

1. Give me totals by Business Unit and by Severity — show each as a chart.
2. Build a Pareto chart of Root Causes. What are the top 3 root causes driving 70%+ of incidents?
3. Calculate, by Business Unit:
   - Incident count
   - High + Critical incident count
   - Average downtime in minutes per incident
   - % of incidents involving a contractor
   - Total customers affected
   Rank the business units on a simple risk score combining these.
4. Look at the Description text column and cluster the 95 incidents into 5–7 themes. Identify any hidden patterns the structured fields don't capture (for example: are "change management" issues showing up across multiple incident types?).
5. End with a 5-bullet "what we should fix first" recommendation for the Network Operations Council, written for a non-technical audience.

Show every chart inline, and at the end summarise the Python steps you ran so the NOC team can reproduce the analysis.
Watch for during the demo:
  • The code panel: Analyst writes Python live — you can read every line, no black box.
  • Text clustering on the Description column — this is genuinely hard in Excel; trivial in pandas + scikit-learn.
  • "Show your work" surfaces the exact script, so NOC and audit teams can verify and re-run next quarter.
  • Re-runs in seconds when the question changes ("now exclude Near Misses" / "now show only RAN sites").
Try these other du-relevant variants:
  • "Take this 5G cell-performance CSV and find any cell with throughput below 70% of plan for 3+ consecutive weeks. Show me a time-series chart for each."
  • "Analyze our latest employee pulse survey free-text responses. Cluster the comments into themes, score sentiment per theme, and flag the top 3 wellbeing concerns."
  • "From this contact-centre data, flag any team where average handling time grew > 15% for 2+ consecutive months — these are our customer-experience risks."
  • "Here are 200 customer complaint tickets. Categorize them, score severity, and show me the trend over the last 12 weeks."
  • Prompting tip: always tell Analyst what the columns mean in plain language. "Column C is monthly OPEX in AED millions" saves a round-trip — and if a column has dates, say so explicitly.

Word Agent

Copilot in Word

What it does: Copilot inside Word — draft from scratch, rewrite for tone, summarize a 50-page report into 5 bullets, and ground any draft on a specific SharePoint file or document library. Now with Work agents that pull live context from across your tenant as you write.

Best for: memos, policies, briefings, executive summaries, meeting notes, contract reviews, Arabic translations.

Prompt — AI Champions Pilot Memo 
Draft a 1-page internal memo from the du Future Leaders network to the CHRO proposing a 90-day "AI Champions" pilot where 30 young employees (ages 25–35) each adopt one Copilot use case in their day-job and report measured time savings.
Structure: Background → Proposal → Success metrics → Ask.
Tone: confident, concise, aligned to du's ambition to be the UAE's most customer-centric, AI-enabled telecom.
Watch for during the demo:
  • Rewrite button flips tone — formal ↔ conversational — without retyping.
  • Reference button grounds the draft on a specific SharePoint file or doc library.
  • Summarize on any long doc collapses 30 pages into the 5 bullets an Executive Committee member would read.
Try these du-relevant variants:
  • "Open this 40-page technical report and give me a 1-page summary in non-technical language for the executive committee."
  • "Translate this internal memo into formal Arabic suitable for distribution to UAE government stakeholders."
  • "Rewrite this section in du's brand voice — confident, warm, customer-first, no hedging."
  • Prompting tip: paste a paragraph in the voice you want and say "match this style." Copilot mirrors examples better than abstract instructions.

Excel Agent

Copilot in Excel

What it does: Natural-language formulas, PivotTables, forecasts, scenario tables, and charts — all directly inside Excel. Copilot writes the formula and explains it, so you can audit, learn, or override. Where Analyst handles messy data with Python, Excel Copilot is the right tool for clean tabular finance: ratios, growth, forecasts, sensitivities — every output traceable to a visible formula.

Best for: planning, budgeting, sensitivity analysis, segment modelling, board-ready charts, finance close packs.

Use case — FY2024/25 Segment Financial Analysis 
You are my financial analyst. Using the attached file Du_FY2025_Quarterly_Results.xlsx (six du business segments × 8 quarters of Revenue, EBITDA, Net Profit, Capex, and Active Customers), do the following directly in Excel:

1. Add two new columns to the Quarterly Results sheet:
   - EBITDA Margin % = EBITDA / Revenue
   - Capex Intensity % = Capex / Revenue
   Apply conditional formatting: green for margins above the segment's own median, red below.

2. Add a column for quarter-over-quarter revenue growth %, with green/red conditional formatting.

3. Build a chart comparing FY2025 vs FY2024 full-year revenue growth across the six segments — one chart, sorted descending.

4. Identify the two highest-quality growth businesses (highest combined revenue growth and EBITDA margin in FY2025) and write a 2-line explanation of why next to the chart.

5. Flag any quarter where a segment's revenue, EBITDA, or net profit moved more than 1.5 standard deviations from its own 8-quarter mean — these are the outliers worth investigating.

6. End with a 5-bullet "so what" summary, written for an Investor Day follow-up note.

Show every formula you write — I want to be able to audit and reuse this model.
Prompt — Growth Column + Conditional Formatting (quick variant) 
Add a column showing each segment's quarter-over-quarter revenue growth %, with conditional formatting (green positive, red negative).
Prompt — du Home Revenue Forecast 
Build a forecast for du Home revenue for the next 4 quarters based on the historical trend, and show me a chart with the confidence band.
Prompt — ARPU Sensitivity Table 
Run a sensitivity table: if du Mobile Consumer ARPU moves ±5% from the FY2025 average, what's the estimated impact on segment EBITDA, assuming Active Customers and cost base stay constant?
Watch for during the demo:
  • Every formula is visible — Copilot doesn't hide what it wrote.
  • "Explain this formula" turns a complex INDEX/MATCH into plain English — great for learning.
  • Forecast charts include confidence bands by default — boardroom-ready in one click.
Try these du-relevant variants:
  • "Build a 3-statement model for a hypothetical AED 500M fibre-to-the-home expansion — revenue, OPEX, depreciation, NPV at 8% WACC."
  • "Cross-tab our headcount data by department × age band × gender — show percentages, not just counts."
  • "Take this 5G cell-site report and flag any cell with throughput below 70% of plan in the last 4 weeks."
  • Prompting tip: name your tables (Insert → Table → Table Name) before asking. Copilot uses table names in formulas instead of cell ranges — way more readable.

PowerPoint Agent

Copilot in PowerPoint

What it does: Build a full deck from a prompt, a Word doc, or a SharePoint file. Copilot generates structured slides, picks imagery, writes speaker notes, and uses agentic Designer to keep everything on-brand. You can also point it at an existing deck and ask for a redesign.

Best for: investor decks, town-halls, campaign kick-offs, training material, leadership offsites, customer pitches.

Prompt — "du's AI-Native Future" 8-Slide Deck 
Create an 8-slide investor-style presentation titled "du's AI-Native Future: A Future Leaders Perspective".

Slides:
1. Title slide
2. Why now — three megatrends: 5G-Advanced, generative AI, UAE digital economy
3. du's AI proof-points: AI-powered network operations, contact-centre AI, GenAI for sales, smart-city partnerships
4. du's growth platforms: Mobile, du Home, du Tech (Enterprise & ICT), du Pay
5. The opportunity for du's young talent (ages 25–35) — five AI skills to build
6. Proposed "AI Champions" 90-day pilot
7. Success metrics and KPIs
8. Call to action

Use the corporate template and add suitable royalty-free imagery.
Watch for during the demo:
  • Speaker notes auto-written for every slide — you walk in ready to present.
  • Designer on any slide reflows layout, swaps imagery, fixes alignment — one click.
  • "Make it shorter / add a chart / merge slides 4 and 5" works mid-flow without losing your work.
Try these du-relevant variants:
  • "Build a 5-slide investor update for next week's Investor Day based on the attached FY2025 results — one chart per slide, formal tone."
  • "Take this Word memo and turn it into a 6-slide deck I can present in 10 minutes — keep all the numbers."
  • "Generate an Arabic version of slides 2–5 for the bilingual audience."
  • Prompting tip: start in Word, then say "create a deck from this document." The structure transfers cleanly — much better than building from a blank prompt.

Agent Builder — Custom Agent: "du Policy Buddy"

Microsoft 365 Copilot · Agent Builder

What it does: Agent Builder is the no-code agent creation surface inside Microsoft 365 Copilot Chat. In a few minutes, anyone in the business can build a purpose-grounded agent — with custom instructions, your own knowledge sources (SharePoint, files, public sites), and starter prompts — and publish it to themselves, their team, or all of du. No code. No devs. Lives inside Copilot Chat and Teams.

Best for: HR policy bots, onboarding assistants, compliance helpers, expert-knowledge agents, technical-doc Q&A, brand & tone guardians.

Configuration spec — Build the Policy Buddy agent 
In Microsoft 365 Copilot Chat, open Agent Builder and configure a new agent with these settings:

Name:
du Policy Buddy

Description:
Your friendly guide to du HR policies, code of conduct, travel, learning & development benefits, and IT acceptable-use rules.

Instructions:
You are du Policy Buddy — a warm, professional assistant for du employees.
- Always cite the exact policy section you are answering from (document name + section)
- If the answer is not in your knowledge sources, say so clearly and route the user to People Services
- Respond in the language the user asked in (English or Arabic). If asked bilingually, answer in both
- Keep responses to 3–5 sentences unless the user asks for more detail
- Never speculate on a policy you do not have a source for
- For sensitive topics (grievances, harassment, whistleblowing), always direct to the formal People / Ethics channel
- Keep the tone friendly and on-brand for du: warm, customer-first, no jargon

Knowledge sources:
- SharePoint site: "HR Policies"
- SharePoint site: "Code of Conduct"
- Document library: "Employee Handbook"
- Public site: du.ae (for company values and public context)

Starter prompts:
1. How many days of paid study leave am I entitled to?
2. What's the travel policy for regional flights?
3. ما هي سياسة العمل عن بُعد؟
4. How do I nominate myself for the Future Leaders programme?
5. Can I use ChatGPT for work documents?

Publish to: Microsoft Teams (personal chat app), shared with the Future Leaders network.
Sample user questions — test the agent live 
1. How many days of paid study leave am I entitled to?
2. Can I expense business-class flights on regional travel?
3. ما هي سياسة العمل عن بُعد؟
4. What's the process to nominate myself for the Future Leaders programme?
5. Am I allowed to use ChatGPT for work documents?
Watch for during the demo:
  • Build time: from blank → working agent in under 5 minutes.
  • Every answer carries a clickable citation back to the policy source — no hallucination risk.
  • Bilingual handling: same question in Arabic gets the same answer, in Arabic, citing the same source.
  • Where it lives: Agent Builder publishes straight into Microsoft Teams as a personal chat app — employees use it where they already work.
Try these other du agent ideas:
  • NOC Buddy — grounded on incident-management procedures and runbooks; answers "what's the right procedure if I see X on the network."
  • Tender Coach — grounded on the Procurement Playbook; helps employees draft compliant tender responses.
  • Sustainability Q&A — grounded on the du Sustainability Report; lets anyone answer journalist or investor questions consistently.
  • Onboarding Buddy — grounded on the new-joiner handbook; lives in the new starter's Teams from day one.
  • Prompting tip: the Instructions field is where 80% of agent quality lives. Be explicit about tone, length, citation behavior, and what to do when the agent doesn't know.

App Builder — No-code apps in Copilot

Microsoft 365 Copilot · App Builder

What it does: App Builder is the no-code app creation surface inside Microsoft 365 Copilot. It turns a plain-English description into a working web app — with a database, UI, authentication, and a shareable link — in minutes. You iterate by chatting: "add a comments section", "change the colors", "send a Teams notification on approval." It's the kind of thing that used to need a 3-person dev team and a sprint.

Best for: internal tools, surveys, idea hubs, request portals, hackathon prototypes, anything that used to live in a SharePoint list or an Excel "mini-app."

Prompt — Build "du Innovation Hub" 
Build me an internal web app called "du Innovation Hub".

Features:
- du employees aged 25–35 can submit ideas in 3 categories: AI & Digital, Customer Experience, Wellbeing & Culture
- Each idea has: title, description, category, submitter name, expected business impact (Low/Med/High), and an upvote button
- A leaderboard view showing top 10 ideas by upvotes
- A "My ideas" view for the logged-in user
- An admin view to mark ideas as "Under review", "Approved", "Declined"
- Brand colors: du magenta (#EC008C) and a clean white background

Deploy it and give me a link I can share with the Future Leaders network.
Watch for during the demo:
  • The app is real — there's an actual database behind it, not a mockup.
  • The link works on mobile, in Teams, anywhere — no install needed.
  • Each change-request becomes a small new build, deployed in seconds.
Iterate live with prompts like:
  • "Add a comments section under each idea so people can build on each other's thinking."
  • "Add a 'suggested partner' field — link each idea to a potential vendor if relevant."
  • "Send a Teams notification to the admin channel whenever an idea reaches 25 upvotes."
  • "Add an export-to-Excel button on the admin view for monthly review."
  • Prompting tip: describe the user and the job to be done first, then the features. Apps come out cleaner that way.

Cowork — Skill Creation: /execbrief

Cowork · Reusable Skill

What Cowork is: Cowork is Microsoft's agentic workspace — a separate Copilot surface where you build reusable skills and run full multi-agent orchestrations with plain English. It's where you go beyond chat and start automating the rituals your team does every week.

What a Cowork skill is: A reusable, parameterized workflow you teach Copilot once — then trigger forever with a short slash-command from anywhere (Copilot Chat, Outlook, Teams). Think of skills as macros for the AI era: capture your team's repeatable rituals and let anyone run them on demand. They take inputs, follow your exact instructions, and produce consistent output every time.

Best for: executive briefings, weekly status updates, tender-response drafts, shift handovers, network flash reports, monthly Investor Day prep — any task that follows the same structure week after week.

Prompt — Create the /execbrief skill 
Create a new Cowork skill called "execbrief".

Purpose:
When invoked with /execbrief, the skill generates a du-tone executive briefing on any topic, with the length, audience, language, and tone all customizable by the user.

Step 1 — Ask the user for the following parameters (use the defaults if the user doesn't specify):
- Topic of the brief (required)
- Audience: Group CEO / CFO / CHRO / CTO / Board / Department VP / Custom — default: Department VP
- Length: TL;DR / 1-page / 2-page — default: 1-page
- Tone: Formal executive / Confident analytical / Warm conversational — default: Formal executive
- Language: English / Arabic / Bilingual side-by-side — default: English
- Source material: any SharePoint files, Teams channels, Word docs, or web links to ground on (optional)
- Decision asked / next step (required)
- Key data points or numbers the user wants included (optional)

Step 2 — Generate the brief with this structure:
- Bottom Line Up Front: 3 bullets at the very top
- Background: 2–3 sentences of context
- Key facts: bulleted, every quoted number sourced
- Risks & considerations: 2–3 bullets
- Recommendation / Ask: 1–2 sentences with a clear decision request

Step 3 — Produce two outputs:
- A Word doc using the du corporate template (magenta headers #EC008C, yellow accent line)
- A 5-line WhatsApp/Teams-ready summary for forwarding on mobile

Step 4 — Style rules:
- Currency in AED unless the source data is in USD (then keep USD and note it)
- Round figures to 2 significant digits
- Cite every quoted number with the source page or section
- Never invent a number; if data is missing, write "[to confirm]"
- For Arabic output, use formal Modern Standard Arabic suitable for senior stakeholders

Step 5 — Save the skill so anyone on my team can run it again with just /execbrief.
Prompt — Trigger /execbrief with a real ask 
/execbrief
Topic: Q1 2026 progress on the Future Leaders "AI Champions" pilot
Audience: CHRO
Length: 1-page
Tone: Confident analytical
Language: English
Decision asked: Approval to scale from 30 to 150 participants in Q2
Key data points:
- 28 of 30 champions still active at end of Q1
- Average self-reported time savings: 4.2 hrs/week
- Top adopted use cases: Outlook triage, deck generation, Excel forecasting
- Engagement score on internal pulse: 8.4/10
- Projected Q2 cost at 150 participants: AED 280k (licenses already provisioned)
Source material: SharePoint folder "Future Leaders/AI Champions Pilot"
Watch for during the demo:
  • One skill — but every output is different, because the parameters drive the result.
  • Consistent du tone and structure across every invocation, no matter who triggers it.
  • The Word doc lands directly in the user's OneDrive, ready to share.
Other du skill ideas worth building next:
  • /handover — shift / project handover note with status, risks, open items, contacts.
  • /netflash — formatted network incident flash report with incident, immediate actions, customer impact, lessons learned.
  • /tenderprep — initial RFP response outline based on a tender document.
  • /investorprep — weekly Investor Day prep brief, grounded on the latest analyst reports.
  • Customize-as-you-go: once a skill is saved, you can keep editing it — "next time, also include a risk RAG status" or "add a section for required approvals."
  • Share it: once published, anyone on your team just types /execbrief from Copilot Chat, Outlook, or Teams — same skill, instant access.

Cowork — End-to-End Orchestration

Cowork · Multi-Agent

What it does: This is where Copilot stops being a chatbot and starts being a chief of staff. One prompt fans out into a plan, the plan dispatches work to multiple specialist agents running in parallel — Researcher, Analyst, Word, PowerPoint, Outlook — and the results synthesize into a single Loop page you can review on mobile. This is what "agentic" actually means in practice.

Best for: meeting prep, executive briefings, customer-visit prep, end-of-week wrap-ups, board pack assembly, anything that today takes you a whole afternoon of context-switching.

Prompt — "Prep me for the Future Leaders meeting" 
You are my chief of staff. Prep me for tomorrow's du Future Leaders meeting at 10:00.

Do all of this in parallel:
1. Pull the meeting invite and list the attendees and agenda.
2. Researcher: give me a 1-page briefing on the latest du public announcements (last 30 days) so I sound current.
3. Analyst: look at the "du Innovation Hub" app database and tell me the top 3 trending ideas this month, by upvotes and by category.
4. Word agent: draft a 1-page chairperson opening remarks based on items 2 and 3, in a warm and confident tone.
5. PowerPoint agent: generate a 5-slide deck that mirrors the opening remarks, ready to project.
6. Outlook: draft (do not send) a follow-up email to attendees that I can use to share the deck after the meeting.

Bundle everything into a single Loop page called "Future Leaders — 21 May Prep" so I can review on mobile tonight.
Watch for during the demo:
  • The plan: Cowork shows the orchestration plan before executing — full transparency.
  • Parallel execution: six specialist agents running concurrently, not sequentially.
  • Synthesis: one Loop page, mobile-friendly, with every artifact linked.
  • Re-runnable: tomorrow morning, the same prompt runs again with fresh data — no copy/paste between tools.
Try these other end-to-end orchestrations:
  • "Prep me for my customer visit tomorrow: company background, last 6 months of news, our open opportunities, last meeting notes, two suggested talking points."
  • "Build the weekly Future Leaders update: pull this week's activities, summarize the top 3 ideas added, draft the email, post it to the Teams channel as a draft for me to review."
  • "End-of-day wrap-up: summarize today's emails, list what I committed to and to whom, draft tomorrow's top-3 priority list, send it to my own inbox at 6 PM."
  • Prompting tip: always end an orchestration prompt with where the output should land — a Loop page, an Outlook draft, a Teams channel. The "delivery" part is what makes it truly agentic.
