Prompting cheat sheet

Eight habits that turn a good prompt into a great one. Keep these in mind every time you type.

1

Set the role

Start with who Copilot should be: "You are a policy analyst for Sharjah Digital Department…"

2

Be specific about output

Say the format and length: "a one-page brief", "5 slides", "a table with 3 columns".

3

Ground it on your data

Point to a file: "Using the attached SDD adoption data…" so answers are real, not generic.

4

Iterate, don't restart

Refine in the same chat: "make it shorter", "add a chart", "more formal tone".

5

Ask it to show its work

"Explain how you got that number" builds trust and catches mistakes.

6

Give a style example

Paste a sentence you like: "write it in this tone" beats describing the tone.

7

Chain, don't cram

Break big asks into steps: research → analyse → draft → present.

8

Verify high-stakes

Always double-check names, figures, and anything citizen-facing before you share it.

Researcher Agent

Copilot's deep-research mode gathers, compares, and cites sources — perfect for benchmarking Sharjah against the world's best digital governments.

Prompt · Benchmark smart governments 
You are a policy researcher for Sharjah Digital Department. Research how three leading smart-government programmes — Estonia's e-Governance, Singapore's Smart Nation, and the UAE federal digital government — increased citizen adoption of online services. For each, give: the core approach, one measurable outcome, and one idea Sharjah could realistically adapt in 2026. Cite your sources and end with a 3-line "what this means for Sharjah" summary.
Prompt · Digital sustainability angle 
Research how governments measure the environmental impact of going paperless (carbon and paper savings). Summarise the 3 most common metrics, how each is calculated, and give one credible example. I want to compare it to Sharjah Digital Department's paperless results. Keep it under 300 words and cite sources.
👀 Watch for during the demo: Copilot lists its sources as it researches. Click one to show the audience that every claim traces back to a real page — this is what makes it trustworthy.
Try these variants:
  • "Add a comparison table with a column for each country."
  • "Now focus only on ideas that a youth council could help pilot."
  • "Rewrite the summary for a 2-minute spoken introduction."

Analyst Agent

Point Copilot at a spreadsheet and it reads, analyses, and charts the data for you — no formulas required.

Prompt · Find the weak spots (uses the adoption file) 
Using SharjahDigitalDepartment_Digital_Services_Adoption.xlsx, analyse the eight government services. Tell me: (1) which service has the LOWEST digital adoption %, (2) which has the LONGEST average processing time, and (3) whether the two are related. Then recommend two practical actions to move more citizens online. Show a bar chart of digital adoption % by service.
Prompt · Tell the impact story (uses the KPI file) 
Open SharjahDigitalDepartment_Digital_Transformation_KPIs.xlsx. Summarise Sharjah's digital-transformation impact from 2022 to 2025 in five plain-language sentences a 16-year-old would understand. Highlight the single most impressive number and explain why it matters for the community.
👀 Watch for during the demo: Copilot explains how it reached each conclusion and can turn the answer into a chart instantly. Ask "which numbers did you use?" to show it is reading the real cells.
Try these variants:
  • "Rank the services from best to worst digital adoption."
  • "Which government sector saved the most hours in 2025?" (Sector Breakdown sheet)
  • "Draft a 3-bullet insight I could put on a slide."

Word

Turn findings into a polished, government-ready document in seconds.

Prompt · Youth Council briefing note 
Draft a one-page briefing note for the Sharjah Digital Department Youth Council titled "Bringing More Citizens Online in 2026". Using the attached adoption data, include: a short background paragraph, three key findings, three recommendations, and a two-line call to action for young people. Use a professional, optimistic government tone. Add a title and section headings.
👀 Watch for during the demo: Copilot builds the full document with headings and structure — then you can say "shorten the background" or "make the tone warmer" and it edits in place.
Try these variants:
  • "Add an Arabic version of the title and headings."
  • "Turn the recommendations into a checklist."
  • "Rewrite it as a 150-word LinkedIn post."

Excel

Ask Copilot to add formulas, spot trends, and build charts — it does the spreadsheet work for you.

Prompt · Grow the KPI analysis 
In SharjahDigitalDepartment_Digital_Transformation_KPIs.xlsx, add a column that calculates the year-on-year growth for each KPI from 2024 to 2025. Highlight the fastest-growing KPI in green. Then build a clustered column chart comparing Government Savings across 2022, 2023, 2024, and 2025.
👀 Watch for during the demo: Copilot writes real, working formulas — not typed-in numbers — so the sheet still recalculates if the data changes. Click a new cell to show the formula behind it.
Try these variants:
  • "Which KPI grew fastest overall, and by what percentage?"
  • "Add a total row and format all savings as AED."
  • "Make a pie chart of the 2025 Sector Breakdown."

PowerPoint

Go from a one-line idea to a clean, on-brand deck ready to present.

Prompt · Sharjah's digital impact story 
Create a 5-slide presentation for the Sharjah Digital Department Youth Council titled "Sharjah's Digital Impact Story". Slides: (1) Title, (2) Why digital transformation matters, (3) Our impact in numbers — use AED 250M saved, 336M sheets of paper cut, and 15,000 tonnes of CO2 avoided, (4) What's next for 2026, (5) How young people can contribute. Clean, modern design with crimson accents. Keep text short and punchy.
👀 Watch for during the demo: Copilot lays out every slide with titles and talking points. Then ask it to "add a closing thank-you slide in English and Arabic" to show live editing.
Try these variants:
  • "Add speaker notes for each slide."
  • "Make slide 3 a single big-number visual."
  • "Give me a 60-second script to present this deck."

App Builder

Describe an app in plain words and Copilot builds a working prototype — no coding needed.

Prompt · Sharjah Service Finder 
Build a simple web app called "Sharjah Service Finder". A citizen types what they need (for example "renew my business licence" or "pay a traffic fine") and the app returns: the right digital service, the channel to use (Mobile App / Web Portal / Service Centre), and an estimated processing time. Use friendly bilingual labels (English / العربية) and Sharjah crimson as the accent colour. Keep the interface clean and mobile-friendly.
👀 Watch for during the demo: A real, clickable app appears in minutes. Type a citizen request live and let the audience see it respond — this is the "wow" moment.
Try these variants:
  • "Add a search box that filters services as you type."
  • "Show a friendly message if no service matches."
  • "Add a light/dark mode toggle."

Cowork Skill

Build a reusable skill once, then run it again and again with different inputs — Copilot remembers your standard format.

Prompt · Create the "SDD Service Brief" skill 
Create a Cowork skill called "SDD Service Brief". Given the name of a Sharjah government service, it produces a standard one-page brief with these sections: Purpose, Target Users, Digital Channel, Typical Processing Time, and One Improvement Idea. Keep the tone professional and concise. After creating it, run the skill to generate a brief for "Health Card Renewal".
👀 Watch for during the demo: Once the skill exists, generating the next brief takes one line. Run it again for "Traffic Fine Payment" to show how a reusable skill saves time.
Try these variants:
  • "Add an Arabic summary line to the skill's output."
  • "Run the skill for three services and combine them into one document."
  • "Add a 1–5 'digital readiness' score to each brief."

Cowork End-to-End

The finale: one prompt that chains research, analysis, a deck, and an email — showing how Copilot handles a whole workflow.

Prompt · From data to decision, in one flow 
Act as my digital-transformation analyst for Sharjah Digital Department. Work through these steps and show me each one:
1. Review the attached adoption and KPI files.
2. Identify the two services with the weakest digital adoption.
3. Draft a short, practical improvement plan for each (2–3 actions).
4. Build a 4-slide summary deck of your findings.
5. Write a 5-line email to the Youth Council chair summarising what you found and what you recommend.
👀 Watch for during the demo: Copilot works step by step and produces several finished deliverables from a single prompt. Pause after each step so the audience sees the workflow build up.
Try these variants:
  • "Redo step 4 as a one-page infographic instead of slides."
  • "Make the email warmer and add an Arabic greeting."
  • "Add a step 6: three questions to ask citizens to validate the plan."
