Prompting Cheat Sheet

Eight rules for getting better results from Microsoft 365 Copilot every single time.

Tip 01

Set the role

Start with "Act as a…" — Copilot adapts its tone and expertise. "Act as a telecom strategy analyst at e&."

Tip 02

Be specific about output

Tell Copilot the format you want: "Give me 5 bullet points", "Write a 1-page brief", "Create a table with 4 columns."

Tip 03

Ground it on your data

Reference files you've opened: "Based on the eand_Subscribers_Network.xlsx file, tell me…" Copilot reads your context.

Tip 04

Iterate, don't restart

Follow up in the same chat: "Make that shorter", "Translate to Arabic", "Add a section on 5G". Copilot remembers the thread.

Tip 05

Ask it to show its work

Add "Explain your reasoning" or "List your sources" to understand how Copilot reached its answer.

Tip 06

Give a style example

Paste a sentence you like and say "Write in this style." Copilot will match your preferred tone and register.

Tip 07

Chain, don't cram

Break complex tasks into steps across messages. Research first, then draft, then refine — better than one giant prompt.

Tip 08

Verify high-stakes output

Always check numbers, names, and key facts before sharing externally. Copilot is fast — you add the judgment.

Researcher Agent

Use the Researcher Agent to find information from the web, summarise sources, and build briefings — without opening a single browser tab.

Prompt 1 — Competitive Landscape

Act as a telecom strategy analyst at e&. Research the current competitive landscape for telecommunications and digital services in the GCC. Who are e&'s main competitors — including du, STC, Zain, and Ooredoo — and where is each one investing most aggressively in 2025 and 2026? Summarise the top 3 strategic battlegrounds (e.g. enterprise cloud, 5G monetisation, AI services) and tell me where e& appears strongest and where it faces the most pressure. Cite your sources.
👀 Watch for during the demo
Copilot pulling live web sources and citing them inline — this is the Researcher Agent at work. Notice how it attributes each claim to a specific source rather than presenting opinions as facts.
🔄 Try these variants
  • "Score each competitor 1–5 on AI readiness and explain why"
  • "Focus only on enterprise B2B — ignore consumer market"
  • "What risks does e& face if STC expands further into UAE?"

Prompt 2 — Technology Trend Brief

I work in strategy at e& and need to brief our leadership team on where the global telecom industry is heading. Research the top 4 technology trends reshaping telecom operators over the next 3 years — such as AI-native networks, private 5G for enterprise, sovereign cloud, and digital identity. For each trend: explain what it is in plain language, give one real-world example of a telecom operator already acting on it, and assess whether e& is ahead, on par, or behind the curve. Format the output as a structured briefing I can share with senior stakeholders.
👀 Watch for during the demo
How Copilot structures a leadership-ready briefing without any formatting instructions — it infers the right structure from context. Ask it to follow up with "Now turn this into an email to my VP" to show how the output flows into action.
🔄 Try these variants
  • "Add a fifth trend: sustainability and green networks"
  • "Rewrite this for a non-technical audience"
  • "Which trend should e& prioritise first, and why?"

Cowork End-to-End

The big finish. Chain Researcher, Analyst, Word, and PowerPoint together in one orchestrated Copilot workflow — going from raw data to a boardroom-ready deliverable in under 5 minutes.

Full Workflow — Board Meeting Prep

I have an e& board meeting tomorrow on our digital transformation strategy. Help me prepare using the following steps:

Step 1 — Research: Find the latest analyst views (Gartner, IDC, GSMA) on Middle East 5G and AI market growth for 2026–2028. Give me 3 key statistics I can cite.

Step 2 — Analyse: Using the 5G Rollout and Subscribers sheets in my open Excel file, summarise our current performance vs targets. Flag the two biggest gaps.

Step 3 — Draft: Write a 2-page executive summary that combines the market view from Step 1 with our performance data from Step 2. Start with a headline finding, then cover: market opportunity, our position, gaps, and three strategic priorities for H2 2026.

Step 4 — Present: Create a 4-slide summary deck I can present in 5 minutes: (1) Market Context, (2) e& Performance Today, (3) Strategic Gaps, (4) H2 Priorities. Add speaker notes for each slide.

Let's go through these one step at a time.
👀 Watch for during the demo
How Copilot maintains context across all four steps — the statistics from Step 1 appear in the Step 3 document without you having to repeat them. This is the compounding power of Copilot: each step builds on the last.
🔄 Try these variants
  • After Step 2: "Now send this summary to my manager on Teams"
  • After Step 3: "Translate the executive summary into Arabic"
  • After Step 4: "Add a fifth slide with 3 risks and how we mitigate them"
